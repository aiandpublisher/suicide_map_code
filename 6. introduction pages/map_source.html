Suicide Mortality in 2019's England

Introduction

Project Goals & Findings:

This website shows the geospatial distribution of suicides in England by local authority (2019). Overall, coastal areas present higher suicides and rates than inland districts. This finding echoes with previous studies that have identified the widening gap between coastal towns and the rest of England in terms of earnings, employment and education [1][2][3]. Such a distribution pattern of suicides therefore indicates the existence of mental health inequality across England, and calls for more effective suicide interventions and preventions for coastal districts.

Compared with 2018 dataset, whereas the changing data does not present apparent geospatial pattern, it is clear that most districts experienced an increase from 2018 to 2019, which urges local authorities to pay more attention to suicide prevention. Such is our prime aim here.

It could also contribute to the understandings on the relation between suicides and personal wellbeings. Whereas life satisfaction, happiness and sense of worthwhile are found to be negatively associated with suicide numbers [1], their influence to suicide rates is less obvious, which echos with some researches [2][3], calling for more studies on causes of suicide risks [4].

Data Source: Office for National Statistics (ONS)

Boundary Data: Local Authority Districts UK BUC, 12/2020.

Suicides by local authority: Suicides in England and Wales: 2019 registrations, 09/2020.

Mid-year population dataset: 2019 mid-year population estimates by local administrative units, 12/2020.

Well-being Dataset: Estimates of personal well-being from 2018-2019 Population Survey (APS), 10/2019.
